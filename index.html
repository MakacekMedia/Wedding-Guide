Section 07

Season & Venue

Choose the season and setting that matches your vision.

Spring

Apr – Jun

Mild weather · blooms · golden light. Can be rainy — always plan a backup.

Summer

Jun – Aug

Long days · outdoor-friendly · peak season. Highest pricing; heat requires planning.

Autumn

Sep – Nov

Stunning foliage · cooler temps · soft light. Very popular — book 14+ months ahead.

Winter

Dec – Feb

Cosy & dramatic · off-peak savings. Shorter daylight hours.

Hotel ballroomConvenience & scaleIn-house catering only, or outside vendors?

Section 06

The Vows

Your words. Use these prompts and the writing space to find them.

ElementGuidance
OpeningHow you feel standing here, right now. One or two sentences.
The storyA single moment or memory that defined your love. Be specific — vagueness is forgettable.
The promises3–5 personal, concrete vows. "I'll make you tea when you can't sleep" beats "I'll always be there."
The closingYour commitment, stated simply and from the heart.
Length1–2 minutes spoken aloud — roughly 150–250 words. Practice slowly; nerves make people rush.

The one rule

Designate one non-family person as the vendor contact for the entire day. Vendors call them — not you. This single decision changes the whole experience.

The one moment

At some point during the reception, step outside together for two minutes — just the two of you. Look at each other. Take it in. Then go back in and celebrate with everyone who loves you.
